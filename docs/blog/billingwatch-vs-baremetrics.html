BillingWatch vs Baremetrics: Do You Really Need to Pay for Billing Analytics?

comparison baremetrics-alternative self-hosted April 4, 2026 · 5 min read

If you're running a Stripe-powered SaaS and want visibility into billing events — failed charges, churned subscriptions, anomalies — your options have traditionally been "pay Baremetrics $9-$99/mo" or "build it yourself." BillingWatch is the third option: open source, self-hosted, free forever.

Here's an honest comparison of the two.

The Core Trade-off

Baremetrics is faster to get running — OAuth your Stripe account, done in 5 minutes. It has a polished UI, rich MRR analytics, and team features. You're paying for that polish and convenience.

BillingWatch gives you full control — your data stays on your own server, it's free, and you can customize it. The trade-off is you're running it yourself. That means a Docker host and a Stripe webhook endpoint.

Who Should Use BillingWatch

  • Solo developers who don't need team dashboards and want zero subscription cost
  • Early-stage founders monitoring Stripe before $10k MRR — paying $9-$99/mo for billing analytics at early stage is rough
  • Privacy-conscious teams who can't send billing data to third-party SaaS
  • Developers who want to extend it — BillingWatch is MIT licensed, hack it as you need

Who Should Use Baremetrics

  • Teams that need advanced MRR forecasting and cohort analytics
  • Non-technical founders who don't want to manage infrastructure
  • Companies already at $50k+ MRR where the $99/mo is justified by the analytics depth

Setting Up BillingWatch in 15 Minutes

If you want to try BillingWatch:

# Clone and start
git clone https://github.com/rmbell09-lang/billingwatch.git
cd billingwatch
cp .env.example .env
# Add your STRIPE_WEBHOOK_SECRET to .env
docker-compose up -d

# Dashboard at http://localhost:8000
# Stripe webhook endpoint: http://your-server:8000/webhook

You'll need to point a Stripe webhook to your server. If you're running locally, Stripe CLI can forward webhooks to localhost for testing.

Bottom Line

If you're pre-revenue or early-stage: use BillingWatch. Zero cost, you own your data, anomaly detection out of the box. If you're a 10-person team that needs collaborative dashboards and MRR forecasting and you're paying $2k/mo to Stripe, Baremetrics is worth considering.

Most indie hackers and solo founders don't need to pay for this.
