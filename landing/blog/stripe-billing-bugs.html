5 Stripe Billing Bugs That Cost SaaS Companies Thousands

March 2026 - 6 min read

Billing bugs are silent revenue killers. They do not crash your app. They do not trigger alerts. They just quietly drain money from your business or overcharge your customers until they notice and leave.

Here are the five most common ones we see in Stripe integrations, with real dollar impact estimates.

1. Phantom Subscriptions

A customer gets assigned to a plan they never signed up for. Usually happens from misconfigured invoice_settings or incorrect product and price mappings during plan migrations.

Typical cost: 20 customers at $500/month wrong plan = $10,000/month in disputed charges

2. Price Drift on Plan Changes

You update your pricing, but existing customers get charged the old rate or the new rate when they should be grandfathered. Happens when price metadata is not synced during plan changes.

Typical cost: 50 customers at $50 pricing gap = $2,500/month in revenue leakage

3. Duplicate Charge Events

Stripe fires a webhook, your system processes it, then Stripe retries and your system processes it again. Double invoices. Angry customers. Chargebacks.

Typical cost: 50 duplicate charges at $100 = $5,000/month in refunds plus chargeback fees

4. Failed Payment Cascades

A payment fails, your retry logic kicks in too aggressively, and the customer gets hit with 5 failed payment emails in one day. They do not fix their card. They cancel.

Typical cost: 20 churned customers at $200 LTV = $4,000 in permanent revenue loss

5. Usage Metering Gaps

For usage-based billing, gaps in meter reporting mean customers get undercharged (you lose money) or overcharged (they dispute). Usually caused by race conditions in meter submission.

Typical cost: 50 customers at $100 metering error = $5,000/month in billing inaccuracy
